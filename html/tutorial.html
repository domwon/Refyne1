How to Play

The objective of the game is to insert pipes and/or heaters to match the expected output pressure and temperature. Initially, you are given an input pressure and temperature. Your task is to find the correct combination (there is likely more than one!) of pipes and heaters to generate the expected output conditions.

The following diagram will represent the playing field:

This grid will be where you place each of your elements, whether it be a pipe or a heater. You will start at the shaded box next to the input field and place your first move there. After that, legal moves are the surrounding boxes that are shaded around it.

Each chemical engineer is equipped with the required tools to complete their daily tasks on time. Above, you will find yours! (Pipes and heaters).

  • Pipes: Each pipe will decrease the pressure by 1 psi or 2 psi.
  • Heaters: Each heater is defaulted to not affect the temperature (0 ℃) but will decrease the pressure by 1 psi.
    • Bonus!: You can change the generated temperature of each heater by simply clicking on it. Drag the slider to your desired temperature and click "Submit Heat" to update the temperature for that heater.
      • Increasing temperature to 25 ℃ will not affect the pressure (0 psi).
      • Increasing temperature to 50 ℃ will result in an increase in pressure by 1 psi.

Each move that you make will represent the path that the system will run through. Because you cannot work backwards (starting from the output field), plan carefully! However, even chemical engineers make mistakes. If you wish to replace a move, simply drag another element on top of the desired box.

Did something bigger go wrong that a simple replace can't fix? Easy enough. On the right side you will find a restart button. Any hefty mistakes will be taken care of by completely resetting the board. Also on the right side, you will see a check answer button appear, once you have placed on the square closest to the output field. Click this and we'll calculate to see if your system works. :)
